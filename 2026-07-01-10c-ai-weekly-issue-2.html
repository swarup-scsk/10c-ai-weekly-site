Issue #2 · Wednesday, 1 July 2026

This issue covers a new cost-effective agentic model from Anthropic, the EU AI Omnibus agreement, a large-scale AI energy storage deployment, and regulatory moves that could reshape European AI infrastructure.

TL;DR

  1. Anthropic released Claude Sonnet 5, a cheaper model for running autonomous agents. See Headline of the week.
  2. EU negotiators agreed on an AI Omnibus package that clarifies some rules but leaves compliance details uncertain. See Headline of the week.
  3. A real-world deployment of AI-driven energy storage across 15 European factories shows how the technology can scale in manufacturing. See The Briefs.
  4. Austria is lobbying the EU to host Anthropic’s AI systems in Europe after US export curbs. See The Briefs.

Headline of the week

The two most consequential developments this week are a new model that lowers the cost of agentic AI and a regulatory update that affects compliance planning for every European enterprise.

Anthropic launches Claude Sonnet 5 as a cheaper way to run agents A more powerful mid-size model that can plan, use tools, and run autonomously at a level previously requiring larger models. (Source: TechCrunch, 30 June 2026, https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/anthropic-launches-claude-sonnet-5-as-a-cheaper-way-to-run-agents/)

10C take: European enterprises piloting autonomous agents in manufacturing, logistics, and financial services now have a cost-effective model option from Anthropic. This reduces the operational barrier to deploying agentic workflows, especially for clients who need to run many agents at scale. Consultants should help clients evaluate Sonnet 5 against their existing agent frameworks.

EU AI Omnibus Makes Key Changes But Leaves Uncertainties Negotiators from the European Council and Parliament agreed on an AI Omnibus package that introduces changes to the EU AI Act framework but leaves several compliance details unresolved. (Source: Law360, 26 June 2026, https://www.law360.com/articles/2489843/eu-ai-omnibus-makes-key-changes-but-leaves-uncertainties-)

10C take: The Omnibus clarifies some requirements but creates new uncertainties that affect risk assessment timelines. Clients in regulated sectors such as healthcare and financial services need to monitor the final text closely. We should advise them to maintain flexible compliance programs that can adapt to the unresolved details.

The Briefs

Short explanations of the other developments worth knowing this week.

Proof point Turbo Energy and HiTHIUM are deploying 366 MWh of battery storage with AI optimization across 15 European factories for a major ceramic manufacturer. Over 130 MWh has already been installed. (Source: Stock Titan, 24 June 2026, https://www.stocktitan.net/news/TURB/turbo-energy-and-hithium-deploy-ai-driven-energy-infrastructure-v0ci5mg52p6t.html)

Why it matters to us: This real-world deployment shows how AI-driven energy management can scale across multiple industrial sites in Europe. Manufacturing and energy clients can use this as a reference case to build business cases for operational cost savings.

Enterprise and industry Innovaccer and AWS signed a multi-year agreement to move healthcare AI agents from pilot to production using Amazon Bedrock and AWS HealthLake, eliminating procurement bottlenecks. (Source: HIT Consultant, 25 June 2026, https://hitconsultant.net/2026/06/25/innovaccer-aws-strategic-collaboration-healthcare-ai/)

Why it matters to us: Healthcare clients can now adopt agentic AI on a proven platform with FHIR-native interoperability. This reduces integration risk and accelerates deployment timelines for clinical and administrative use cases.

Regulation and governance The proposed EU Financial Data Access Regulation (FiDA) would create a harmonised framework for accessing customer financial data, including insurance data, with significant technology and compliance implications for insurers. (Source: Kennedys Law LLP, 26 June 2026, https://www.kennedyslaw.com/en/thought-leadership/article/2026/insurance-meets-open-finance-what-fida-means-for-insurers/)

Why it matters to us: Financial services clients must prepare for data-sharing obligations under FiDA, which will affect their AI model training, customer analytics, and third-party data partnerships. We should help them assess their current data access practices.

Regulation and governance Austria is pushing the EU to host Anthropic’s AI systems within Europe to counter US restrictions on foreign access to advanced models, reflecting broader European concerns about AI infrastructure dependency. (Source: Global Banking & Finance Review, 28 June 2026, citing Bloomberg News, https://www.globalbankingandfinance.com/austria-lobbies-eu-host-anthropic-ai-us-curbs-bloomberg-news/)

Why it matters to us: If the EU hosts frontier AI models, European enterprises could gain sovereign access to advanced capabilities, altering procurement strategies and reducing reliance on US-based cloud providers. This is a long-term trend worth monitoring.

Models and tools ⚠️ OpenAI and Broadcom jointly released the custom AI inference chip "Jalapeño" on June 24, 2026, according to a community forum post. No official company announcement was found in the provided sources. (Source: Moomoo community, 26 June 2026, https://www.moomoo.com/community/feed/openai-launches-first-self-developed-ai-inference-chip-boosting-nvidia-116813940457481) ⚠️ This information is from an unverified forum post and should be treated as unconfirmed.

Why it matters to us: If confirmed, a custom inference chip from OpenAI would reduce its dependence on NVIDIA hardware and potentially lower cost per query for enterprise customers, affecting long-term AI infrastructure planning. We should treat this as a rumour until official confirmation.

Enterprise and industry PitchBook launched a federated Copilot connector that brings private capital market data into Microsoft 365 Copilot, Copilot in Excel, and Copilot Chat for secure enterprise AI use. (Source: Yahoo Finance via Business Wire, 25 June 2026, https://sg.finance.yahoo.com/news/pitchbook-expands-premium-ai-integrations-131500268.html)

Why it matters to us: Financial services clients can now query private market data directly within their existing Microsoft 365 workflows, reducing friction in AI adoption and improving analyst productivity. This is a concrete example of how AI tools are embedding into everyday enterprise software.

Proof point ⚠️ STELIA’s VP of Applied AI argued that the biggest shift in enterprise AI is from experiments to real-world applications, highlighting that scaling requires business transformation beyond technology. (Source: Los Angeles Times (LA Times Studios content), 26 June 2026, https://www.latimes.com/0000019f-04aa-df06-a99f-97ead2d50000-123) ⚠️ This content was produced from press releases and contributor content, not independent news reporting.

Why it matters to us: This industry perspective reinforces that client projects must focus on organisational change and process redesign, not just piloting AI tools, to achieve production-scale impact. It aligns with our consulting approach.

Client lens

This week’s developments reinforce two themes for our client work. First, the cost of deploying agentic AI is dropping with models like Claude Sonnet 5, making it viable for clients who previously could not justify the expense. Second, the regulatory environment is becoming more concrete but also more ambiguous: the EU AI Omnibus and FiDA both signal that compliance obligations are coming, but the details are still shifting.

For manufacturing and energy clients, the Turbo Energy/HiTHIUM deployment provides a strong proof point for AI-driven energy optimisation. For financial services clients, the PitchBook-Microsoft integration and FiDA developments create both new capabilities and new compliance burdens. In healthcare, the Innovaccer-AWS partnership shows a clear path to production agentic AI. Consultants should use these examples to help clients move from pilot paralysis to scoped production projects.
